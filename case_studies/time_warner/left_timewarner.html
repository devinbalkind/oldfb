Entertainment

Background

Readers of annual reports should be actively engaged; they should be entertained and stimulated, much as they are by consumer business publications like Forbes, Business Week, and Fortune. Does the financial community read or pitch out the narrative section? We believe the answer lies in what is said and how it's communicated.There's no reason to read an annual report that simply regurgitates old news. If, however, the report provides a sense of where the company is going -- helping to clarify the vision of top management -- there's every reason for the analyst or investor to pay attention.

The Time Warner 1989 annual combined entertainment value with the answer to the questions most on the minds of the financial analysts - Why did they merge? - resulting in extraordinary readership. Through this book, we were able to position Time Warner as neither Time nor Warner, but as a new type of entertainment company, on the cutting edge of the industry.
