  The "Re-Introduction" of Knight Ridder

While Knight Ridder includes such well known publications as The Miami Herald, Philadelphia Inquirer, Detroit Free Press and San Jose's Mercury News among the 31 newspapers it owns, the corporation has long kept a low profile and been perceived as a "local" news organization. To expand awareness and gain the national recognition it deserves as a quality news, information and customer service organization, Knight Ridder has launched a branding campaign, now underway. The campaign is comprised of a new corporate identity, web site and image advertising, all developed by Frankfurt Balkind.

Identity

At the heart of the "reintroduced brand" is a new identity, which is being applied to the front pages of the Knight Ridder newspapers, some 31 in all, as well as applied to all news racks, vehicles such as delivery trucks, and all forms of communication from print to online. Recognizing the importance of each community newspaper's own local identity, the new Knight Ridder signature is unobtrusive but dynamic as it begins to build a more visible corporate brand.

Advertising Campaign

The new advertising campaign, breaking in May, creates awareness for Knight Ridder as a national news organization with local roots -- one that specializes in creating relevant content for its audiences and distributing it in whatever medium they prefer. The tagline is "Information for Life" and the campaign can be seen in print publications including Business Week, Forbes, AdWeek, MediaWeek, BrandWeek and Fortune, as well as locally in all 31 Knight Ridder newspapers.

Website

As part of the integrated branding effort, we also updated and re-designed the Knight Ridder corporate Website. The re-design is part of a larger effort to update and broaden the site's navigational architecture.
