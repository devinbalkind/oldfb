Changing Perceptions

Background

In order to generate ad sales and increase circulation in the American marketplace, The Economist needed to reach media buyers and sophisticated business professionals, many of whom felt the publication was somewhat boring, particularly when they were used to the more visually exciting American magazines.

Strategy and Execution

Our strategy was to change the perception of The Economist from a boring "English" read to a stimulating international business publication. We ran print ads in upscale consumer publications and to the trade, and avoided showing the magazine's somewhat dull cover, but instead showcased its masthead. We then used provocative headlines and images to communicate that the magazine was interesting and even "spicy" - a definite read for the business professional who needed to be in the international know.

Results
According to the publisher, this campaign was "by far the most successful" they had ever run in America, increasing awareness levels, generating excitement and advertiser interest substantially from previous years.
