Enhancing Appreciation
of a Cultural Institution

Background

Founded in 1933, the San Francisco Ballet Company is Americas oldest professional ballet company, and under Helgi Tomasson, it has become one of the best directed and most important dance companies in the world.

While San Franciscos War Memorial Opera House was undergoing earthquake retrofitting, the San Francisco Ballet was displaced for two years. They would make a return in December 1997 in time for the Nutcracker Season performances. In an effort to broaden awareness and enthusiasm for the Ballet, we were asked to help re-position the institutions identity. The plan was to unveil the new identity in conjunction with the Ballets "return home."

The Challenge

To develop an identity that would emphasize San Francisco Ballets commitment to the "classical" training of its dancers (which gives them extraordinary capabilities) while also communicating the Ballets innovative and thrilling dance program. In addition, we wanted to send a message that while the San Francisco Ballet is a "world class" ballet, it is "accessible" to a broad, diverse audience.

The Solution: The Axis Concept

The graphic expression of the Ballet identity uses a "classic" typeface for the words "San Francisco" to convey the Ballets proud tradition and its classic training. A contemporary, sans serif typeface for "Ballet" speaks to the bold, forward-looking and innovative vision of the Ballet. The axis format has the flexibility to provide a classical, yet bold new cornerstone for a wide range of dynamic and broadly appealing imagery.
