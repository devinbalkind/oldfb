Marketing an Office Tower

Background

Fisher Brothers wanted to build, and, subsequently, market Park Avenue Plaza as a prestigious office tower in New York City. While the building was named Park Avenue Plaza, its entrances were actually on East 52nd and 53rd Streets between Park and Madison.

Frankfurt Balkind first helped Fisher Brothers obtain financing with a brochure that gave a tangible reality to the development. Once the structure was underway, we set about carving out an identity that would differentiate this office tower from others in Manhattan by building upon the caché of Park Avenue. This included creating an identity with newly designed letter forms and logotype, leasing brochures, advertising (image, retail and commercial leasing, and retail co-op), press kits, stationery, computerized signage system, ad infinitum. We later created retail sales kiosks in the public space, along with a special retail newsletter and exhibits for the Christmas season.

Results

Park Avenue Plaza was 90% leased before construction was completed, attracting such prestige corporate clients as First Boston, Reliance Group, McKinsey & Company, and Elizabeth Arden company.
