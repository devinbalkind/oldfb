Interactive Drama featuring Full-Motion Video

Background

Sneaks is a full motion video game that takes place in a NYPD Blue-style urban juvenile detention unit inhabited by young, "hip," cops involved in a wide range of cases: missing persons, gang wars, drug busts, drive-by shootings, and potential suicides, etc. The player assumes the role of Sneaks, one of the unit's teenage detectives. Using a proprietary engine, the game delivers rapid-paced action and full-motion video that test the player's hand/eye coordination and puzzle-solving skills. Accompanied by a sound-track featuring hit tunes, the game conveys the look and feel of the inner city.

In focus groups, players enjoy feeling as if they are inside the "head" of the detective, and interacting with "real" people -- not the cartoon characters of games like Carmen Sandiego. "It's a brain teaser," one said, "much superior to shoot-em-ups." And kids are able to suspend disbelief -- several described the game as "interactive TV" -- "like a cop show, but you're doing it."
