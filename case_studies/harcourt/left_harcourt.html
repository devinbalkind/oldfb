Launching a New Reading Program

Background

Once every four to five years, educational publishers go out into the marketplace — state-by-state — in the hopes of winning one of the coveted 5-6 year contracts for the state adoption of their reading, science or math programs. Harcourt Brace commissioned us to help market their "Treasury of Literature" series in Texas, one of the country's three most important markets, worth approximately $110 million.

Strategy

Our target: the educational committees, teachers and others who control purchasing of school programs. Our strategy: to generate awareness of the new reading program prior to and during product sampling; to differentiate the product from the competition's; to establish the "Treasury of Literature" program as the gold standard.

Execution

We were involved with every aspect of the marketing program — advertising, product packaging and design of key elements, collateral materials, brochures and flyer, exhibit. The creative across the board was extremely teacher-friendly, not only in its appealing use of colors and graphics, but also in its ease-of-use, be it a yellow "brick" road guiding prospects through the exhibit, design devices to direct the user through the materials, or graphics on premiums and on outdoor billboards illustrating the theme, "Where the love of reading begins."

Results

The "Treasury of Literature" program captured an unprecedented 47% of the Texas reading market and was subsequently used successfully in other states.
