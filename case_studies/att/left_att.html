AT&T's Alternate Channels is made up of three groups, each of which targets different business-to-business audiences regarding the advantages of working with and using AT&T for hosting and commerce services. We created a unified brand with three sub-brands -- each one of them appropriate for and focusing on the benefits their specific audience(s) care about most.

The Creative Alliance Program (CAP) appeals to creative agencies and software developers who work with client companies on building their Web sites. We created the CAP site to help them easily join the Program. The home page directs users to a Gallery & Studio showing other members' work, a relevant case history, program information, and a quick way to become a member.

You must be registered with CAP to go into the Member's Only area, which is a unique platform created to provide exclusive information, build community and give members the tools they need to help them sell.

The Marketing Alliance Program (MAP) is focused on three different audiences -- independent agents, corporate decision-makers, and resellers -- all of whom can use AT&T advantageously. Three statements on the welcome page quickly send the user down a distinct path, based upon their individual interests.

A combination survey/registration questionnaire generates leads for AT&T while giving corporate decision-makers information about the Web they can use based upon their industry, size, geographical location, and interests.

The Global Alliance Marketing site aggressively makes their point to VARS (Value Added Retailers) who sell electronic messaging -- "Use AT&T and you can grow your market and build repeat business! Then, the site makes it easy to join."

Results
The Alternate Channels site was launched at Comdex the week of November 17th. Early response has been enormously positive with more than 200 new members joining.
