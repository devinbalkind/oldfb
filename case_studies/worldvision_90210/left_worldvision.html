Broadening Appeal

Background

The objective of the campaign effort behind Beverly Hills 90210 was to announce the availability of the show for syndication and garner the greatest possible price by communicating the phenomenal ratings success and audience appeal of the show. While the audience delivery numbers alone were quite impressive, it was critical that the sales materials, video and trade advertising campaign go beyond communicating the audience research. The show needed to be sold to the target audience of station general managers and cable network programmers who were unfamiliar with it or had false preconceived notions that it was solely a trendy teen show. In addition, the one-hour dramatic format suggested a problem for scheduling.

Our strategy was to leverage the ratings success of Beverly Hills 90210, both in year-to-year growth and in the demographic growth of the viewing audience (beyond the teen market), to include other important audience segments including young males 18 - 24 and women 18 - 34. We positioned the show as a sleeper that became one of the hottest shows on TV because people of all ages related to the universal and timeless appeal of its characters and storylines. All communications carried the theme "Beverly Hills 90210. We all live there."
