Positioning a Leader and Building a Brand

Background

Consistently profitable, Seagate is the world's leading independent manufacturer of hard drives, with revenues and profits exceeding those of all other independent drive manufacturers combined. Yet, from a business perspective, the industry's spotty earnings and "commodity product" image has kept Seagate's price-to-earnings ratios low.

In terms of its products, Seagate needed to differentiate itself and advance its position in the marketplace. One of the ways they were achieving the latter was through acquisitions.

Seagate wanted nothing less than to dominate data management software solutions. To accomplish this they needed: 1) clarity as to who they were and what their products were and; 2) higher awareness among their audiences. Based upon strong core positioning of Seagate as the company that scores breakthrough solutions, we developed and executed an integrated program across media to begin building their brand.

Overall Results

The stock price doubled; sales increased; and awareness is higher. Advertising

We created a print and internet image campaign that asks an attention-getting question. It reminds readers/users that you can't get the right answer without the right information and generates word-of-mouth among the high tech audiences Seagate is after - CIO's, IS executives, database administrators and LAN/WAN managers. Ads run in 12 publications including PC Magazine, PC Week, Information Week, and InfoWorld. Annual Report

For the annual report, our goal was to communicate that with the high barriers to entry in the industry, there would be few new competitors, thereby, raising Seagate's P/E. The report charts the knowledge base, R&D investments and breakthrough solutions that have made Seagate the industry's undisputed leader.

To grab the reader, we used an aggressive visual format, presenting pop culture images and globally-relevant "factoids" to make the complex world of storage accessible. One example: "Imagine flying on an F-16 at Mach 813, 1/62nd of an inch above the ground, and landing on a blade of grass. We do that as often as 100 times a second."

Vision Video

In a video journey, the viewer learns that more "human" computer communications will require vast amounts of storage -- much more than the 500 - 1000 megabytes packaged in most new PCs today. Developed for broad audiences, internal & external, this video ponders the simple question, "What good is it if a computer can't communicate like you and me?"; i.e. through moving images [video], voice [sound] and natural languages. Borrowing from the music video genre, video follows global images of people interacting with a suitcase -- a metaphor for storage -- underscored by hypnotic music commissioned for the piece.

Capabilities Video

Conceived as a follow-up to the "vision" video, this piece provides specific facts about Seagate's capabilities and success. Featuring a spokesperson who "tours" various locations and supplies factoids, the video is used in sales presentations, new employee orientation and background briefings.
