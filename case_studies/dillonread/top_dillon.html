Using The Annual Review As A Strategic Tool

The 1992 Dillon Read Annual Review broke from tradition, applying a strategic focus. Instead of using the book to simply outline firm history and services - stating facts - we used it as an opportunity to clarify Dillon Read's positioning as a client-focused organization.

Pointing to the choices Dillon Read has made in working with clients and conducting business, a comparison between the company and its primary competitors was developed in five sections: People, Custom-Tailored, Balance, Focus, Ownership. For example:

People

Senior people whose primary responsibility is managing and growing the resources of the business vs. Senior people who continually refine their craft by remaining hands-on with client concerns.

Custom-Tailored

Financial products that appeal to broad market segments vs. Customized solutions designed to resolve each client's specific problem or opportunity.

Case studies inset as a box in each section further reinforce the firm's business choices and the benefits they have brought to their clients.
