Brand Strategy

Naming & Identity

To us, an identity isn't just a name and a graphic expression. It's the starting point for all your thinking about your brand. We look to create brands that can become part of the culture, that create a framework for the strategic ideas that motivate both internal and external audiences.

Adobe Acrobat. About.com. The Surge Economy.
We create names to be powerful tags that can both set expectations and focus behavior. We also develop nomenclature systems that take you from corporate brand to product families to names for individual family members. With nomenclature determined, we develop the identity design-the logotype, symbol if one is used, and how it's all applied-to bring that name to life. Most importantly, an identity needs to have strategic ambiguity. It must be strategic enough to stand for who you are and what you want to be, but ambiguous enough to allow for growth and change. When appropriate, we conduct research in the information-gathering stage prior to creative development. We may also formally test executions that need to work across languages and cultures. Because we're experienced executing in all media, our applications systems are uniquely sensitive. We structure these systems to encourage using each medium to its best advantage. Put another way, our application systems focus more on what you can do than on what you can't do. Their goal is not just surface consistency, but inner coherence.
